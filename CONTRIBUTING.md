# Contributing to LeanBridge

Thank you for your interest in contributing to LeanBridge! We welcome contributions from the
community and appreciate your efforts to improve the project. Please follow the guidelines below
to ensure a smooth contribution process.

## How to contribute

1. **Find an issue to work on.** Browse the
   [open issues](https://github.com/CBirkbeck/LeanBridge/issues). Each one describes a definition or
   theorem from the [LMFDB](https://www.lmfdb.org/) to connect to Mathlib, and is labelled by area
   (e.g. `number-fields`, `modular-forms`, `abelian-varieties`) and difficulty (e.g.
   `level: intermediate`, `level: advanced`). Pick one that matches your interests and experience.

2. **Say you're working on it.** Leave a comment on the issue so we avoid duplicated effort, and ask
   there if the goal or scope is unclear.

3. **Do the work.** This usually means adding the definition or theorem to Mathlib if it isn't there
   yet, then linking the relevant LMFDB knowl to it. See
   [Linking to Mathlib](README.md#linking-to-mathlib) in the README for the full workflow and the
   tools for finding the right Mathlib declaration.

4. **Open a pull request.** When your work is ready, open a PR against `main` and reference the issue
   it addresses (e.g. "Closes #66"). Describe what you did and flag anything left as `sorry` or
   `axiom`. Draft PRs are welcome for work in progress.

Small fixes — typos, documentation, golfing a proof — are very welcome too. For those, feel free to
skip straight to step 3 and open a PR directly.
